Job Search Strategy Plan:

This comprehensive strategic plan outlines my approach to securing a data science or machine learning engineering position through targeted applications, strategic networking, and continuous skill development.

Career Objectives

Primary Target Roles:

  • Data Scientist (Mid-Level)
  • Machine Learning Engineer
  • AI Engineer
  • Applied Scientist

Industry Focus: Technology (AI/ML products, SaaS), Healthcare Tech (Medical AI), FinTech (Risk modeling, Fraud detection), Consulting (Technical consulting, AI strategy)

Geographic Preferences: Primary: Boston, MA | Secondary: San Francisco Bay Area, Seattle, NYC | Open to: Remote opportunities

Timeline & Milestones

Month 1-2: Foundation Building

  • Week 1-2: Optimize all application materials (resume, portfolio, LinkedIn)
  • Week 3-4: Build portfolio website showcasing TailorTrack and key projects
  • Week 5-6: Initiate informational interviews with 10 industry professionals
  • Week 7-8: Apply to 15-20 target companies, attend 2 industry events

Month 3-4: Active Searching

  • Apply to 20-25 positions per month
  • Continue networking: 5 new connections per week
  • Prepare for technical interviews: LeetCode, ML system design
  • Attend virtual career fairs and company-specific events

Month 5-6: Interview Phase

  • Focus on companies in advanced interview stages
  • Conduct mock interviews with peers and mentors
  • Negotiate offers strategically
  • Evaluate opportunities based on growth potential, culture fit, compensation

Target Companies

Tier 1 (Dream Companies): Tech Giants (Google, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, Apple), AI-First (OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere, Scale AI), High-Growth (DataBricks, Snowflake, Weights & Biases)

Tier 2 (Strong Fit): Established Tech (ServiceNow, Datadog, Salesforce), Healthcare AI (PathAI, Tempus, Insitro), FinTech (Stripe, Plaid, Robinhood), Consulting (McKinsey Digital, Deloitte AI, Accenture AI)

Tier 3 (Growth Stage): Series B-D startups in AI/ML space, Boston-area tech companies with strong ML engineering teams

Application Strategy

Quality Over Quantity Approach:

  • Deep Research (30 min per company): Understand product, tech stack, recent news; identify specific ML/AI teams; find relevant employees to connect with
  • Customized Applications: Tailor resume to highlight relevant experience; write personalized cover letters for top 40 companies; reference specific projects/initiatives
  • Multi-Channel Application: Company website (primary), Employee referrals (most effective), LinkedIn Easy Apply (selective), Specialized ML/AI recruiting agencies
  • Application Tracking: Maintain detailed spreadsheet with company, role, date applied, status, contacts; set reminders for follow-ups

Networking Strategy

Northeastern University Network:

  • Connect with 50 Khoury College alumni working in data science
  • Attend Northeastern career fairs and industry panels
  • Leverage professor connections at Institute for Experiential AI
  • Join Northeastern Data Science club and AI research groups

Professional Networking:

  • LinkedIn Strategy: Optimize profile with portfolio projects; post weekly about ML projects and industry insights; send 10 personalized connection requests weekly to professionals in target roles
  • Industry Events: Attend 2 conferences/meetups per month (NeurIPS, CVPR, Boston ML Meetup, PyData Boston, AI Alignment Forum)
  • Informational Interviews: Schedule 2-3 per month with professionals at target companies; prepare thoughtful questions about team culture, tech stack, growth path
  • Referral Strategy: Target 40% of applications through referrals; leverage BCG and KPMG alumni networks

Skills Development & Interview Preparation

Weekly Goals:

  • Complete 5 LeetCode problems (focus on medium difficulty)
  • Study 1 ML system design case study
  • Read 2 technical papers on recent ML advances

Interview Preparation:

  • Coding Interviews: Master common algorithms (trees, graphs, dynamic programming); practice explaining thought process clearly
  • ML System Design: Study cases (recommendation systems, search ranking, fraud detection); understand trade-offs (accuracy vs. latency, complexity vs. maintainability)
  • Behavioral Interviews: Prepare 8-10 STAR method stories covering leadership, teamwork, handling failure, technical challenges, cross-functional collaboration

Success Metrics

Weekly Targets: Applications: 5-7 | New connections: 10 | Coding problems: 5 | Learning hours: 10

Monthly Targets: Total applications: 20-25 | Informational interviews: 3-5 | Interview requests: Target 10% response rate

Overall Goal: Secure offer for data science/ML engineering role by June 2025 with competitive compensation ($100K-$130K base for internship/co-op, $120K-$160K for full-time), strong mentorship and learning opportunities, meaningful work in AI/ML applications, and clear career growth path.

Contingency Plans

If Search Extends Beyond 6 Months:

  • Expand geographic search to include all major tech hubs
  • Consider contract/consulting roles to build experience
  • Pursue additional certifications (AWS ML Specialty, TensorFlow Developer)
  • Contribute to open-source ML projects for visibility

Alternative Pathways: Research Assistant positions at Northeastern (Institute for Experiential AI), Part-time ML roles while continuing studies, Freelance data science consulting
